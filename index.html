About

myself

I always thought my dream was to be an editor for a Kenyan publishing company, sipping coffee and reading opening lines to the next best-selling novel. I agitated towards editorial career and even started a bachelor's course in publishing and media studies. But I yearned to learn more, I yearned to learn something different. And that's when it happened; the spark I was missing ignited the instant I clicked run on my first Java program. Since then I can't get enough. Java development calls to all my passions; it incorporates creativity and problem solving and am allowed to do web development (using spark ofcourse). The passion carried me through my education in moringa school. Here there were more resources available to me than I could have ever imagined. I studied new languages, algorithms, compilers, version control, I learned how to work in teams and collaboration using git. I also improved my soft skills in presentation as I got experience from the several project presentations and idea pitching I had. I even worked as a scrum master in a group of four for a web application. It was here that I learned truly how much there was yet to learn. So, now, in industry, the passion remains with me. My specialties include quickly learning of new skills and programming languages, problem solving, responsive design principles, website optimization and the model view controller(MVC) methods of organizing code. So far I have JavaScript, HTML, CSS, bootstrap, jQuery, postgreSQL, Java, spark, Android development, git& GitHub under my belt. I have started learning angular.js and kotlin, I am still enthusiastically grabbing onto any other programming languages, frame works or principles I can integrate into the knowledge I already have.
